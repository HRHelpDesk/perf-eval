EMPLOYEE PERFORMANCE EVALUATION

An employee performance evaluation, also known as a “performance review,” is a process used by employers to provide employees with feedback on their job performance and officially document that performance feedback.

Companies use a variety of evaluation cycles with the most popular being to conduct employee performance evaluations at least once per year.

360 PERFORMANCE EVALUATION

A 360 degree performance evaluation is a process in which employees receive confidential, anonymous feedback from the people who work with and/or around them. This typically includes the employee's manager, peers, and direct reports.

PERFORMANCE IMPROVEMENT PLAN

A performance improvement plan (PIP), also known as a performance action plan, is a tool used to give an employee the opportunity to correct performance efficiencies and succeed in their current position.

Goal setting and accountability are the keys to a successful PIP. A PIP may be used to address an employee’s failure to meet specific job goals and/or to address and correct behavior-related concerns.

UPWARD EVALUATION

An upward evaluation (UE) provides an opportunity for subordinates to evaluate their manager’s performance and hopefully provide constructive feedback that can be used to improve the effectiveness of a manager and help them better understand the needs of the team they lead. UEs can be conducted anonymously or in the open.

Anonymous evaluations are often more productive and informative as there is no fear of reprisal on the part of the employee submitting the evaluation.
